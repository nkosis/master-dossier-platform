INTRODUCTION
Purpose of This Dossier

Throughout the Scriptures, one of the most serious charges made by YAHAWAH (LORD) against His people is that they were stiffnecked. This description appears from the days of MASHA (Moses), through the prophets, and into the Renewed Covenant.

To be stiffnecked is not ordinary stubbornness. It is resistance against correction, commandments, repentance, and the authority of YAHAWAH (LORD). It is the elevation of personal desire, pride, and human reasoning above the will of ALAHAYAM (God).

This interactive dossier examines what a stiffnecked people are, how rebellion manifests, what judgment follows rebellion, how the righteous follow the will of YAHAWAH (LORD), and why many are called but few are chosen.

Core teaching: The difference between rebellion and righteousness is not merely hearing the word. The difference is obeying it.

FOUNDATIONAL FRAMEWORK
Key Definitions and Teaching Lens
What Is a Stiffnecked People?

A stiffnecked people are stubborn, rebellious, resistant to correction, and unwilling to submit unto the commandments of YAHAWAH (LORD). The image is of an animal refusing to bend its neck to the yoke. Spiritually, it describes people who refuse the yoke of obedience.

Why Obedience Matters

The covenant relationship between YAHAWAH (LORD) and His people is not built on personal preference. It is governed by commandments, judgments, statutes, correction, repentance, and covenant responsibility.

The Difference Between Called and Chosen

Many can hear the truth, receive warnings, and be exposed to evidence. The chosen are those who endure in obedience, correction, repentance, and righteousness.

SECTION 01
The Nature of a Stiffnecked People

This section defines the term stiffnecked from the direct scriptural charge made by YAHAWAH (LORD) against His people.

01YAHAWAH Identifies the People as Stiffnecked
Exodus 32:9▼
SCRIPTURE - 1611 KJV RENDERING

And YAHAWAH (LORD) said unto MASHA (Moses), I have seen this people, and, behold, it is a stiffnecked people:

MODERN RENDERING

And YAHAWAH (LORD) said unto MASHA (Moses), "I have seen this people, and they are stubborn and rebellious."

UNDERSTANDING

This statement comes after the golden calf rebellion. The people had witnessed deliverance, signs, commandments, and the power of YAHAWAH (LORD), yet they still turned aside. A stiffnecked people are not ignorant people; they are people who know correction and resist it.

02Favor Was Not Given Because of Their Own Righteousness
Deuteronomy 9:6▼
SCRIPTURE - 1611 KJV RENDERING

Understand therefore, that YAHAWAH (LORD) thy ALAHAYAM (God) giveth thee not this good land to possess it for thy righteousness; for thou art a stiffnecked people.

MODERN RENDERING

Understand that YAHAWAH (LORD) your ALAHAYAM (God) is not giving you this good land because of your own righteousness, because you are a stubborn people.

UNDERSTANDING

This verse removes self-righteousness. Covenant favor came through the promise and mercy of YAHAWAH (LORD), not because the people were naturally obedient. The warning is direct: blessing must not be mistaken for personal merit.

03The Charge Is Repeated
Deuteronomy 9:13▼
SCRIPTURE - 1611 KJV RENDERING

Furthermore YAHAWAH (LORD) spake unto me, saying, I have seen this people, and, behold, it is a stiffnecked people:

MODERN RENDERING

YAHAWAH (LORD) spoke again and said, "I have seen this people, and they are stubborn and rebellious."

UNDERSTANDING

The repeated accusation confirms that stiffnecked rebellion was not a single incident. It was a spiritual pattern. When rebellion becomes habitual, it must be confronted directly by the word of YAHAWAH (LORD).

SECTION 02
Rebellion Against Commandments

This section shows how stiffnecked behavior appears in action: pride, refusal to hear, refusal to obey, and generational hardening.

04Rebellion Was Known Beforehand
Deuteronomy 31:27▼
SCRIPTURE - 1611 KJV RENDERING

For I know thy rebellion, and thy stiff neck: behold, while I am yet alive with you this day, ye have been rebellious against YAHAWAH (LORD); and how much more after my death?

MODERN RENDERING

I know your rebellion and stubbornness. Even while I am alive with you today, you have rebelled against YAHAWAH (LORD); how much more after my death?

UNDERSTANDING

MASHA (Moses) identifies rebellion as already active while instruction was still present. This proves that proximity to leadership, miracles, and commandments does not automatically produce obedience.

05Pride Hardened the Neck
Nehemiah 9:16-17▼
SCRIPTURE - 1611 KJV RENDERING

But they and our fathers dealt proudly, and hardened their necks, and hearkened not to thy commandments,

And refused to obey, neither were mindful of thy wonders that thou didst among them; but hardened their necks, and in their rebellion appointed a captain to return to their bondage: but thou art a ALAHAYAM (God) ready to pardon, gracious and merciful, slow to anger, and of great kindness, and forsookest them not.

MODERN RENDERING

They and our fathers acted proudly, hardened their necks, did not listen to Your commandments, and refused to obey. They did not remember Your wonders, but in rebellion wanted to return to bondage. Yet You are ALAHAYAM (God), ready to pardon, gracious, merciful, slow to anger, and full of kindness.

UNDERSTANDING

This passage defines the mechanics of rebellion: pride, refusal to hear, forgetfulness, disobedience, and a desire to return to bondage. Yet it also reveals the mercy of YAHAWAH (LORD), who remains ready to pardon when repentance is real.

Consequences of Rebellion

This section shows the required correction and the spiritual danger of resisting the RAWACH QADASH.

07The Command to Stop Being Stiffnecked
2 Chronicles 30:8▼
SCRIPTURE - 1611 KJV RENDERING

Now be ye not stiffnecked, as your fathers were, but yield yourselves unto YAHAWAH (LORD), and enter into his sanctuary, which he hath sanctified for ever: and serve YAHAWAH (LORD) your ALAHAYAM (God), that the fierceness of his wrath may turn away from you.

Do not be stubborn like your fathers were. Submit yourselves to YAHAWAH (LORD), enter His sanctuary, and serve YAHAWAH (LORD) your ALAHAYAM (God), so His fierce wrath may turn away from you.

The remedy is clear: stop being stiffnecked, yield, enter properly, and serve. The text ties repentance to the turning away of wrath. Obedience is not optional; it is the path away from judgment.

SECTION 04
Following the Will of YAHAWAH

This section turns from the problem to the solution: following YAHAWAH (LORD) instead of personal understanding, emotion, or desire.

09Do Not Lean on Personal Understanding
Proverbs 3:5-7▼
SCRIPTURE - 1611 KJV RENDERING

Trust in YAHAWAH (LORD) with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding.

In all thy ways acknowledge him, and he shall direct thy paths.

Be not wise in thine own eyes: fear YAHAWAH (LORD), and depart from evil.

MODERN RENDERING

Trust in YAHAWAH (LORD) with all your heart. Do not rely on your own understanding. Acknowledge Him in all your ways, and He will direct your paths. Do not be wise in your own eyes. Fear YAHAWAH (LORD), and depart from evil.

UNDERSTANDING

The stiffnecked person trusts self. The righteous person trusts YAHAWAH (LORD). This passage directly commands the reader not to rely on personal understanding above divine instruction.

SECTION 05
Perfect Submission

This section shows that true obedience requires doing the will of THE FATHER, not merely speaking religious words.

13Doing the Will of THE FATHER
Matthew 7:21▼
SCRIPTURE - 1611 KJV RENDERING

Not every one that saith unto me, Lord, Lord, shall enter into the kingdom of heaven; but he that doeth the will of my Father which is in heaven.

MODERN RENDERING

Not everyone who says, "Lord, Lord," will enter into the kingdom of heaven, but the one who does the will of THE FATHER in heaven.

UNDERSTANDING

Verbal profession is not enough. Entrance into the kingdom is tied to doing the will of THE FATHER. This directly opposes empty religious speech without obedience.

SCRIPTURE - 1611 KJV RENDERING

Overcoming the World

This section shows how worldly lust, pride, and desire oppose obedience.

SCRIPTURE - 1611 KJV RENDERING

Love not the world, neither the things that are in the world. If any man love the world, the love of THE FATHER is not in him.

For all that is in the world, the lust of the flesh, and the lust of the eyes, and the pride of life, is not of THE FATHER, but is of the world.

And the world passeth away, and the lust thereof: but he that doeth the will of ALAHAYAM (God) abideth for ever.

Do not love the world or the things in the world. If anyone loves the world, the love of THE FATHER is not in him. The world feeds rebellion through lust, pride, and desire. The obedient overcome the world by doing the will of ALAHAYAM (God).

Many Are Called, Few Are Chosen

SCRIPTURE - 1611 KJV RENDERING

For many are called, but few are chosen.

MODERN RENDERING

Many are called, but few are chosen.

SCRIPTURE - 1611 KJV RENDERING

Enter ye in at the strait gate: for wide is the gate, and broad is the way, that leadeth to destruction, and many there be which go in thereat:

Because strait is the gate, and narrow is the way, which leadeth unto life, and few there be that find it.

Enter through the narrow gate, because wide is the gate and broad is the way that leads to destruction, and many enter through it. Narrow is the way that leads to life, and few find it.

The broad path represents the easy path of rebellion, comfort, and conformity. The narrow path requires discipline, correction, and obedience.

The last shall be first, and the first last; many are called, but few are chosen.

CONCLUSION
Final Understanding

The Scriptures repeatedly show that stiffnecked rebellion leads to judgment. YAHAWAH (LORD) rebuked His people because they resisted correction, refused commandments, hardened their hearts, and trusted their own desires.

The solution is not mere religious speech. The solution is repentance, obedience, humility, and submission to the will of YAHAWAH (LORD). YAHAWASHA (Jesus) demonstrated perfect submission when He said, "Not my will, but thine, be done."

Many are called, but few are chosen. The difference is proven by obedience, endurance, and faithfulness. The righteous do not overcome rebellion by following the world. They overcome by doing the will of ALAHAYAM (God).

Summary: A stiffnecked people hear correction and resist it. The chosen hear correction and submit to it.
